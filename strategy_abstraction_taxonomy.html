GPU Compute · Market Structure

Abstraction Layers in GPU Infrastructure

Four distinct buying and operating models — each representing a different tradeoff between control, complexity, and cost. Rows ordered from least to most abstracted.
Hyperscaler Owns data centers and GPU infrastructure at massive scale. Sells compute as a managed cloud service. AWS, Google, Microsoft.
Neocloud Owns and operates GPU-specialised data centers. Purpose-built for AI workloads. Does not offer the breadth of a hyperscaler.
AI Lab / Model Provider Trains and serves foundation models. Does not own GPU infrastructure — leases compute from hyperscalers and neoclouds. Sells model output via API.
Platform / Aggregator Software layer that routes to, or abstracts over, underlying compute. Does not own infrastructure. Competes on developer experience and tooling.
