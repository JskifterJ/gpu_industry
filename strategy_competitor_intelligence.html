Buyer Routing Guide: Where Different Personas Source Compute
🔵 Layer 5 (Model-as-a-Service / Inference APIs)
No-ops consumers using hosted inference. Together.ai is the primary option—token-based billing, managed endpoints, zero infrastructure overhead. Pricing: ~$0.50–2.00 per million tokens depending on model size.
🟢 Layer 3–4 (Managed IaaS / GPU Clusters)
Enterprise IT teams and AI startups wanting dedicated hardware. CoreWeave, Nebius, Lambda, Crusoe, AWS, Azure are options. Reservation discounts critical (40–60% cheaper than on-demand). Buyers: foundation model labs, enterprise AI teams.
🟡 Layer 1–2 (Bare-Metal / HPC)
Research labs and large model companies. Owners only: CoreWeave, Nebius, Lambda, Crusoe. Supply guarantees matter more than price. Buyers: DeepSeek, Mistral, xAI, academic labs. Differentiation: SUNK/Soperator, InfiniBand topology, health check quality.
🔴 Cost-Sensitive (Budget Training)
Startups with limited CapEx. Vast.ai ($1.49–1.87/h), Voltage Park ($1.99/h), SF Compute ($1.45/h) are the floor. Caveat: spot/aggregator models = reliability trade-off. Job restarts can offset savings.
🟣 Sovereign/Compliance (EU/GDPR)
Nebius, Scaleway, GCORE, DataCrunch/Verda. Buyers: European enterprises, financial services, regulated sectors. Pricing: ~10–20% premium over US equivalents for EU data residency. Nebius + Soperator = best SonK option for GDPR-sensitive teams.
⚫ Hyperscaler Lock-In (Existing AWS/GCP/Azure)
AWS, Azure, GCP, Oracle. Buyers: enterprises already married to one cloud. Advantage: unified storage/networking, compliance certifications. Disadvantage: 2–4× more expensive than neoclouds, egress fees lock you in.
