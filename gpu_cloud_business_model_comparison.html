Key Takeaways: How Buyers Source Compute Across the Stack

Layer 5 (Model-as-a-Service / Inference APIs): "No-Ops" consumers (ChatGPT users, non-technical business users) source via REST APIs. Providers: OpenAI, Anthropic, Together.ai (serverless), Google Gemini. Pricing driver: Token generation, not GPU-hours.

Layer 3–4 (Managed Inference / IaaS): ML engineers + MLOps teams buying dedicated GPU capacity. Providers: CoreWeave, Nebius, Lambda, Crusoe, AWS, Azure, GCP (managed K8s). Pricing driver: Reservation length (on-demand vs 1-yr reserved = 40–60% discount), network topology (InfiniBand premium), orchestration sophistication (SUNK vs standard Slurm).

Layer 1–2 (Bare-Metal / HPC Training): Large research labs + foundation model companies source raw hardware. Differentiation: guaranteed supply (CoreWeave via NVIDIA partnership), software optimization (FluidStack's Atlas OS, Together.ai's TKC kernels), or geographic arbitrage (Nebius EU sovereign, DataCrunch renewable energy premium, Crusoe stranded gas).

Buyer Personas: Researcher (on-demand, spot pricing: Lambda, Vast.ai); Startup AI team (self-service neocloud: Nebius, Voltage Park, RunPod); Enterprise (compliance: Azure FedRAMP, Nebius GDPR, CoreWeave SOC 2); Frontier lab (wholesale bespoke: CoreWeave, FluidStack, Oracle).

Competitive Moats: Commodity pricing losers will be Vast.ai / Akash clones ($0–1/hr). Winners: (1) Verification (Gensyn's proofs), (2) Sovereign compliance (Nebius EU, Azure DoD), (3) Software specialization (Together.ai kernels, CoreWeave SUNK), (4) Energy arbitrage (Crusoe, Genesis Cloud renewable).
