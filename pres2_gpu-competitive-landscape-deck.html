Historical Edge of Orchestrators
Inference kernel innovation: FlashAttention, PagedAttention, and SGLang originated in the orchestrator/research community — not at hyperscalers. These cut inference cost by 5–24× before hyperscalers built equivalent optimisations.
Open-weight model hub integration: Orchestrators were first to offer Hugging Face, LangChain, and open-weight model integrations — creating the fastest deploy paths before any managed ML service offered comparable speed.
Software-above-GPU category creation: Orchestrators invented the layer between raw GPU infra and application code — fine-tuning pipelines, serverless inference, experiment tracking — before managed ML services matured.
Pure vs Hybrid Orchestrator
Pure Orchestrators (Modal, Run:ai, W&B): Zero hardware. Scheduling intelligence, experiment tracking, multi-cloud federation. 100% software margin.

On-Demand vs Reserved — most aggregators offer both
Most aggregators offer both on-demand (hourly spot) and time-scheduled reservations. The key difference from a neocloud: no cluster SLA in either case. Reserved aggregator = guaranteed schedule. Reserved neocloud = guaranteed schedule + SLA + health checks.
Moat
Supply breadth (more providers → better price discovery) · Unified billing · Developer trust · Zero capex = fast unit economics
Risk
Supply tightens → pricing leverage collapses · Cluster quality "Russian roulette" · No SLA = enterprise can't build production · Hyperscalers undercut with reserved pricing
Historical Edge of Aggregators
Zero-friction access: Aggregators created the first no-commitment, per-hour GPU market at a time when every alternative required multi-month reservations with procurement cycles. This unlocked a category of users that neoclouds and hyperscalers couldn't serve.
P2P supply discovery: Vast.ai pioneered real-time bidding for idle capacity from crypto miners, creating a supply pool that traditional cloud providers cannot tap. The result: RTX 4090 at $0.30/hr — structurally impossible through any other channel.
Transparency layer: Aggregators built the first public price comparison across providers — a function that previously required sales calls and NDA-bound proposals, democratising GPU procurement research.
Aggregator vs Orchestrator — Key Distinction
Aggregator routes at the GPU level. Your team manages everything above L3. The aggregator's job ends at provisioning.
Orchestrator manages the software layer above the GPU. Scheduling, experiment tracking, inference serving, developer APIs. Value-add is in L4–L7 software.

Emerging Business Models · Next Archetypes
Five new archetypes emerging that don't fit cleanly into the four current models — the stepping stones toward Deck 3 startup positioning.
🏛️
Sovereign AI Cloud
Early · Growing Fast
Government-owned or government-contracted GPU infrastructure. Nations building AI compute independence. France (OVHcloud), Saudi Arabia (Humain/SoftBank), UAE (G42), India (Neysa).
Driver: EU AI Act + data sovereignty laws force local compute. Governments cannot rely on US hyperscalers for sensitive workloads.
🔬
Vertical AI Stack
Emerging
Full-stack AI provider with compliant compute, domain models, and workflow integration for a single vertical: healthcare (HIPAA+BAA+clinical LLMs), legal, or financial services.
Driver: Regulated industries can't use public APIs for sensitive data. Domain models outperform general models. Bundling is the key to enterprise stickiness.
⚡
Inference-First Cloud
Now · Rapid Growth
Infrastructure purpose-built for agentic inference, not training. Prefill-decode disaggregated clusters. Hardware mix of GB200 UltraServers (inference) + H100 SXM (training) in same facility.
Driver: Inference is already 65–80% of GPU-hours. Agentic workloads multiply tokens-per-task. Training-optimised clusters waste 40%+ efficiency on serving.
🔗
Compute Finance
Nascent · High Potential
GPU compute as a financial asset. RPO-backed debt, GPU futures, institutional asset management. Ornn Compute went live in 2025. CoreWeave IPO validated compute as institutional collateral.
Driver: Top neoclouds hold $400B+ collective RPOs. Morgan Stanley, BlackRock actively underwriting GPU infrastructure debt.
🌐
Edge AI Fabric
Speculative · 3–5yr
Distributed inference at network edge — telco PoPs, CDN nodes, enterprise on-prem interconnected into a unified inference fabric. Sub-20ms latency for real-time AI.
Driver: Cloud inference adds 50–200ms. Real-time AI requires <20ms. 5G enables edge compute economics. Telcos have fibre + PoP estate but lack AI expertise.
📊
Compute Market Infrastructure
Live · Early Liquidity
Standardised compute units, futures contracts, securitised hardware purchases, and RPO-backed debt. Ornn futures went live 2025. Goal: liquid, exchange-traded GPU capacity market.
Driver: Newest silicon remains rationed — information asymmetry a liquid market could resolve. $400B+ collective RPOs validate compute as institutional asset class.
